### **Why Gather/Eat Dominates**

The gather/eat strategy is a **reward-maximising equilibrium** given the game mechanics:

1. **Gather is always valid,** unlike hunt (requires Animal entity + optionally Spear), flee (requires Danger entity), or mitigate (requires Danger), gather can be performed on any encounter. It has zero risk and always yields some resources.
2. **Eat is always valid,** as long as you have any food (berries, fish, cooked meat), eat restores energy. Since you gather food constantly, you always have something to eat.
3. **The strategy is self-reinforcing**: `gather → accumulate food → eat → restore energy → survive → gather more`. This loop has no risk and a moderately positive reward.
4. **Context-dependent actions have higher variance,** hunting when an Animal is present yields high reward but requires the right tool. Fleeing when Danger is present is critical but only relevant ~25% of the time (10/40 entities are Danger). The learning signal for these conditional strategies is sparse.

### **Why the Agent Can't Break Out**

The gradient dynamics create a trap:

1. **SoftMax concentration**
    1. Once `gather` has slightly higher logits than other actions, SoftMax amplifies this, if gather has logit 2.0 and hunt has 1.0, the probability ratio is $e^2/e^1≈2.7×$. After a few epochs: logit 5.0 vs 1.0 → ratio  $e^5/e^1≈55×$.
2. **Gradient starvation for rare actions**
    1. When $p(hunt) < 0.01$, the gradient $∂L/∂z_{hunt}$
     is proportional to $p(hunt)$ and becomes negligible. Even if hunting would give 10× the reward, the gradient is 10× smaller than for gather.
3. **The entropy bonus is a blunt instrument**
    1. It pushes probability mass toward *all*  11 actions equally, including useless ones like endure (which is always bad). What we actually need is to push mass toward *contextually appropriate* actions, not toward uniform exploration.
4. **Communication-action coupling**
    1. The receiver must learn two things simultaneously: 
        1. decode the message to know what entity it faces
        2. map that entity knowledge to the right action. 
    2. In the current architecture, the same network does both. When the action head collapses to gather/eat, there's no gradient signal telling the receiver *which* entity-message pairs should trigger *which*  alternative actions.

### **Fundamental Issues**

1. **Reward structure**
    1. Gather gives reliable + small reward. Hunt gives occasional + big reward but frequent + 0 or - penalty. The agent correctly learns the risk-adjusted strategy is to always gather, unless we make the penalty for *not hunting when you should* much larger or the reward for successful hunting much larger.
2. **Missing reward shaping**
    1. There's no explicit penalty for using gather when a Danger entity is present and unaddressed. The "unaddressed danger" penalty is implicit (danger reduces health at turn end), but it's temporally delayed and not attributable to the specific action.
3. **Single-step RL**
    1. The agent sees one entity and picks one action. It can't plan a multi-step strategy like "gather wood now to craft spear later for hunting." The episode-level survival rate integrates over 20 turns, but each action decision is independent.
4. **No advantage estimation**
    1. In standard RL, we compute advantages $A(s,a)=Q(s,a)−V(s)$ to tell the agent whether an action is better than average. Our loss uses raw rewards, so "gather gives +2" looks equally good regardless of whether "hunt would have given +5."
