For the sake of this dissertation, I have designed two survival games that take two different approaches for the data source. One is an image based implementation and the other is vector based.

# **GAME 1: Island Survival (Image Based)**

## **Core Concept**

Two agents cooperatively survive on an island through sequential decision-making stages. Agent 1 (Scout) perceives visual threats/opportunities, communicates to Agent 2 (Strategist), who decides actions affecting both agents' survival.

## **Game Structure**

### **Stages** (5-10 rounds per episode)

Each stage presents a survival scenario:

1. **Threat Detection** (animal encounters)
2. **Resource Gathering** (food or materials)
3. **Crafting Decisions** (tools or shelter)
4. **Environmental Events** (weather or disasters)
5. **Health Management** (injuries or energy)

The stages will be randomly allocated, simulating nature and avoiding helping the AI agents success.

### **Agent Roles**

**Scout (Sender)**:

- Receives: 1 image (28x28 or 64x64)
- Produces: Variable-length message (vocab_size=50-100 to be decided)
    - Message length be 2/3
- Perceives the current situation but cannot act

**Strategist (Receiver)**:

- Receives: Message from Scout + (optionally) partial context (such as existing tools, weather…)
- Produces: Action choice (2-5 discrete options)
- Makes decisions that affect survival (could maybe have a third agent decide?)

### **Image Sources**

**Option A: Pre-rendered Symbolic Images**

- Create simple 28x28 or 64x64 grayscale/color images representing:
    - Animals: Lion, deer, fish, snake… (8-10 animals)
    - Resources: Tree, water, berries, rocks… (8-10 resources)
    - Weather: Sun, rain, storm, snow… (4-6 weather)
    - Tools: Spear, fire, shelter icons… (6-8 items)
- **Generation**: Use PIL/OpenCV to programmatically draw simple shapes and icons
    - https://pillow.readthedocs.io/en/stable/index.html
    - https://medium.com/jungletronics/opencv-image-basics-2e63d973851a
    - Have chatgpt or gemini pro generate images. Maybe sora
- With this method we have full control, infinite generation and it is compositional (can combine elements)
- **Size**: ~100 base templates, can generate millions of combinations

**Option B: Real Image Dataset** (Advanced)

- Use CIFAR-10 (animals) or miniature ImageNet
    - https://www.cs.toronto.edu/~kriz/cifar.html
    - https://www.image-net.org/index.php
- Filter/curate specific categories
- This makes our images rich and realistic, but it is harder to control distribution, requires more compute power.

### **Action Space Per Stage**

**Example: Encounter Lion Stage**

Scout sees: `[Lion image]` → Sends message → Strategist chooses:

1. **Run** (Safe, -20 energy, 90% survival)
2. **Fight** (Risky, -40 energy, 60% survival without weapon, 85% with spear)
3. **Hide** (Medium, -10 energy, 75% survival)
4. **Ignore** (Dangerous, -5 energy, 30% survival)

**Example: Find Berries Stage**

Scout sees: `[Berry bush image]` → Sends message → Strategist chooses:

1. **Gather** (+30 energy, 95% safe berries, 5% poisonous)
2. **Cook first** (+50 energy, 100% safe, requires fire, -10 energy)
3. **Ignore** (0 energy change)

### **State Tracking**

Agents carry state across stages within episode:

- **Energy**: 0-100 (dies at 0)
- **Inventory**: `{has_spear, has_fire, has_shelter}` (binary flags)
- **Health**: 0-100 (affects survival probabilities)

### **Reward Structure**

**Immediate Rewards**:

- Survival: +10 per stage survived
- Energy gain: +0.1 × energy_gained
- Tool crafted: +20
- Death: -100 (episode ends)

**Long-term Rewards**:

- Complete episode (10 stages): +100 bonus
- Optimal path bonus: +50 (if found efficient crafting chain)
- Communication efficiency: -0.1 × message_length (encourages concise messages)

**Training Set**: 40k episodes, test: 10k, evaluation: 10k

**Why This Studies EC**

1. **Context Dependency**: Same image (lion) requires different messages based on inventory state
2. **Compositional Pressure**: Must develop concepts for {animal-type, threat-level, required-tool}
3. **Sequential Coherence**: Messages across stages must maintain consistency
4. **Emergent Referentiality**: Will agents develop symbols for specific animals vs. threat levels?
5. **Efficiency Pressure**: Shorter messages save "energy," encouraging compression

# **GAME 2: Resource Chain Survival (Vector-based)**

## **Core Concept**

Two agents must collaboratively survive by managing resources, hunting animals, avoiding dangers, and building tools. Animals serve as both threats and food sources, creating risk-reward trade-offs that require clear communication.

## **Game Structure**

### **Input Representation**

Each entity (resource/animal/event) represented as a discrete attribute vector:

**Vector Format**: 

- `[entity_type, subtype, danger_level, energy_value, tool_required, weather_dependency]`

### **Example vectors**

**Animals**:

- Lion (aggressive):
    - `[0, 0, 3, 8, 1, 0]` (entity=animal, subtype=predator, high danger, high energy reward, spear required, any weather)
- Deer (passive):
    - `[0, 1, 1, 6, 1, 0]` (entity=animal, subtype=herbivore, low danger, medium energy, spear required, any weather)
- Snake (hidden):
    - `[0, 2, 2, 3, 0, 0]` (entity=animal, subtype=reptile, medium danger, low energy, bare hands ok, any weather)
- Fish:
    - `[0, 3, 0, 4, 2, 0]` (entity=animal, subtype=aquatic, no danger, medium energy, fishing tool required, any weather)
- Rabbit (fast):
    - `[0, 4, 0, 3, 1, 0]` (entity=animal, subtype=small-game, no danger but hard to catch, low energy, spear required, any weather)

**Resources**:

- Raw meat (from hunt):
    - `[1, 0, 3, 2, 0, 0]` (entity=resource, subtype=food, high danger-parasites, low energy raw, no tool to gather, any weather)
- Cooked meat:
    - `[1, 0, 0, 7, 3, 0]` (entity=resource, subtype=food, no danger, high energy, fire required to create, any weather)
- Wood:
    - `[1, 1, 1, 0, 0, 1]` (entity=resource, subtype=material, low danger-splinters, no energy, no tool, sunny weather preferred)
- Berries:
    - `[1, 2, 1, 3, 0, 0]` (entity=resource, subtype=food, low danger-some poisonous, medium energy, no tool, any weather)
- Stone:
    - `[1, 3, 0, 0, 0, 0]` (entity=resource, subtype=material, no danger, no energy, no tool, any weather)
- Water:
    - `[1, 4, 0, 2, 0, 0]` (entity=resource, subtype=water, no danger if clean, small energy boost, no tool, any weather)

**Environmental Dangers**:

- Storm:
    - `[2, 0, 2, -5, 4, 2]` (entity=environment, subtype=weather, medium danger, energy loss, shelter required, rain weather)
- Cold night:
    - `[2, 1, 1, -3, 3, 0]` (entity=environment, subtype=temperature, low danger, energy loss, fire required, any weather)
- Poisonous plant:
    - `[2, 2, 3, -8, 0, 0]` (entity=environment, subtype=hazard, high danger, major energy loss, no tool helps, any weather)

**Tools/Crafted Items**:

- Spear (craftable):
    - `[3, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0]` (entity=tool, subtype=weapon, enables hunting, made from wood+stone)
- Fire (craftable):
    - `[3, 1, 0, 0, 0, 0]` (entity=tool, subtype=utility, enables cooking, made from wood)
- Shelter (craftable):
    - `[3, 2, 0, 0, 0, 0]` (entity=tool, subtype=protection, protects from weather, made from wood)
- Fishing rod (craftable):
    - `[3, 3, 0, 0, 0, 0]` (entity=tool, subtype=weapon, enables fishing, made from wood)

### **Dimensions Explained**:

1. **Entity Type** (6 values):
    - 0 = Animal (living creature, can be hunted)
    - 1 = Resource (gatherable item)
    - 2 = Environmental danger (cannot be gathered, must be avoided/mitigated)
    - 3 = Tool (craftable, provides capabilities)
    - 4 = Special event (rare bonuses/challenges)
    - 5 = Reserved for future expansion
2. **Subtype** (5 values per entity type):
    - For animals: predator, herbivore, reptile, aquatic, small-game
    - For resources: food, material, water, medicine, fuel
    - For environment: weather, temperature, hazard, terrain, disaster
    - For tools: weapon, utility, protection, weapon, container
3. **Danger Level** (4 values):
    - 0 = Safe
    - 1 = Low risk (5-10% injury chance)
    - 2 = Medium risk (20-30% injury chance)
    - 3 = High risk (40-60% injury chance or death)
4. **Energy Value** (10 values):
    - -5 to -1 = Energy drain (negative outcomes)
    - 0 = Neutral
    - 1-8 = Energy gain (food/successful actions)
5. **Tool Required** (5 values):
    - 0 = None (bare hands)
    - 1 = Spear (hunting weapon)
    - 2 = Fishing rod
    - 3 = Fire (for cooking/warmth)
    - 4 = Shelter (for weather protection)
6. **Weather Dependency** (3 values):
    - 0 = Any weather
    - 1 = Sunny weather only (gathering wood, hunting easier)
    - 2 = Rain weather (fishing better, some dangers)

**Total unique entities**: 6 × 5 × 4 × 10 × 5 × 3 = 18,000 possible combinations (most invalid, ~200-300 valid/meaningful entities)

### **Agent Roles**

**Agent 1 (Scout/Resource Identifier)**:

- Sees: Current encounter (1 entity vector) + current survival state + 2-3 distractor entities
- Sends: Variable-length message (vocab_size=100-150, to be decided)
- Task: Identify which entity/action is optimal given current tools, energy, and dangers

**Agent 2 (Decision Maker)**:

- Sees: Message from Scout + current survival state (energy, inventory, weather)
- Chooses: Action from options that depend on encounter type

### **Action Space by Encounter Type**

*When encountering an animal*:

1. **Hunt** (requires appropriate tool, risk injury, gain meat)
2. **Avoid/Run** (safe, costs energy, no reward)
3. **Observe** (learn behavior, small energy cost, future advantage)
4. **Trap** (if have materials, delayed reward, medium risk)

*When encountering a resource*:

1. **Gather** (collect raw resource)
2. **Gather & Process** (if have tools, get processed version)
3. **Ignore** (save energy for later)
4. **Store** (if have inventory space)

*When encountering danger*:

1. **Use tool to mitigate** (fire for cold, shelter for storm)
2. **Endure** (take damage, save tools)
3. **Flee** (high energy cost, avoid damage)

*Crafting actions* (available when having materials):

1. **Craft spear** (wood + stone → enables hunting)
2. **Start fire** (wood → enables cooking, warmth)
3. **Build shelter** (wood × 2 → weather protection)
4. **Make fishing rod** (wood + patience → enables fishing)

### **Survival Mechanics**

**State Variables**:

- **Energy**: 0-100, decreases by -8-12 per turn (random variation for challenge)
- **Health**: 0-100, decreases when taking damage from animals/dangers
- **Inventory**:
    - Tools: `{spear: bool, fire: bool, shelter: bool, fishing_rod: bool}`
    - Materials: `{wood: 0-5, stone: 0-3, meat: 0-3}`
- **Weather**: Changes every 4-6 turns randomly
- **Turn counter**: Episode = 15-25 turns (variable length)
- **Hunt success rate**: Depends on tool, animal danger level, and health

### **Animal Encounter Mechanics**

When Agent 2 chooses to hunt an animal:

```python
def hunt_outcome(animal_danger, has_spear, agent_health, agent_energy):
    base_success = 0.3  # Without tools
    if has_spear and animal_danger <= 1:  # Easy prey
        success_rate = 0.85
    elif has_spear:  # Dangerous prey
        success_rate = 0.65
    else:  # No weapon
        success_rate = 0.20
    
    # Health/energy affect performance
    success_rate *= (agent_health / 100) * (min(agent_energy, 50) / 50)
    
    if random() < success_rate:
        # Success: gain meat, small injury risk
        meat_gained = animal_energy_value
        injury_chance = animal_danger * 0.15
        energy_cost = 15
    else:
        # Failure: injury risk, no meat
        meat_gained = 0
        injury_chance = animal_danger * 0.4
        energy_cost = 20
    
    if random() < injury_chance:
        health_loss = 10 + (animal_danger * 10)
    
    return meat_gained, health_loss, energy_cost
```

### **Danger Encounter Mechanics**

Environmental dangers have specific mitigation strategies:

- **Storm** + no shelter → -15 health and -10 energy
- **Storm** + shelter → -2 energy (safe inside)
- **Cold night** + no fire → -10 health and -8 energy
- **Cold night** + fire → +5 energy (warmth bonus)
- **Poisonous plant** → 50% chance of -20 health if gathered without knowledge

### **Transformation Chains** (agents must discover)

1. **Basic Survival**:
    - Hunt animal → Raw meat → (with fire) → Cooked meat (+7 energy vs +2 raw, eliminate parasite risk)
2. **Tool Progression**:
    - Gather wood + stone → Craft spear → Hunt efficiently (success rate: 30% → 70%)
    - Gather wood → Start fire → Cook food (2× energy value) + Warmth (+5 energy in cold)
    - Gather wood ×2 → Build shelter → Weather protection (eliminate storm damage)
3. **Advanced Strategies**:
    - Fish (low danger, consistent food) requires fishing rod (wood)
    - Multiple hunts possible with one spear (durable)
    - Fire can cook multiple meats (batch processing)

### **Reward Structure**

**Survival Rewards**:

- Stay alive: +10 per turn
- Maintain high energy (>70): +5 per turn
- Maintain high health (>70): +3 per turn
- Successfully hunt animal: +15 + (danger_level × 10) [harder prey = more reward]
- Complete transformation chain: +40 (e.g., hunt → cook → eat cooked meat)
- Discover new tool: +50
- Episode completion (reach turn 20+): +150
- Efficiency bonus: +(remaining_energy + remaining_health) / 2

**Penalties**:

- Energy drops to 0: -50 (each run because of starvation to death)
- Health drops to 0: -300 (injury death)
- Failed hunt with injury: -20
- Eat poisonous/parasitic food: -30
- Inefficient resource choice: -8 (e.g., gathering berries when have cooked meat available)
- Message too long (>8 symbols): -2 per extra symbol
- Wasted action (e.g., hunt without tool): -10

**Communication Efficiency Bonus**:

- Shorter messages that lead to correct decisions: +1 per saved symbol

### **Key Challenges for Emergent Communication**

1. **Entity Type Distinction**: 
    - Must differentiate animals (huntable) vs resources (gatherable) vs dangers (avoidable)
2. **Risk Communication**: 
    - How to express "high reward but dangerous" (lion) vs "safe but low reward" (berries)?
3. **Tool Dependencies**: 
    - "This animal REQUIRES spear" vs "Can hunt but risky without spear"
4. **State-Dependent Optimal Actions**: 
    - Same animal encounter has different optimal actions depending on:
        - Current energy (low energy → avoid risky hunts)
        - Available tools (no spear → can't hunt lion)
        - Current inventory (already have meat → don't hunt)
        - Weather (storm coming → prioritize shelter materials)
5. **Sequential Planning**: 
    - "Gather wood NOW to craft spear for LATER hunt"
6. **Compositional Pressure**: 
    - Need symbols for:
        - Animal types (predator, herbivore, etc.)
        - Danger levels (safe, risky, deadly)
        - Tool requirements (needs-spear, needs-fire)
        - Urgency (immediate-threat vs opportunity)

**Dataset Splits**:

- **Training**: 80k episodes (~1.6M individual encounters)
- Testing: 20k episodes (~400k encounters)
- **Validation**: 20k episodes (~400k encounters)
